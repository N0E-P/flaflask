Velocipede

Velocipede is a term used to describe a human-powered land vehicle with one or more wheels. The most common type of velocipede is a bicycle. The term is derived from the Latin words "velox" meaning "swift" and "pedis" meaning "foot".

Velocipedes were invented in the early 19th century. The first velocipede was a two-wheeled vehicle that was propelled by the rider pushing off the ground with their feet. This design was later improved upon with the addition of pedals and a chain drive system that allowed the rider to propel the vehicle by turning the pedals with their feet.

Today, velocipedes are a popular form of transportation and recreation. They are used for commuting, exercise, and sport. There are many different types of velocipedes, including road bikes, mountain bikes, and recumbent bikes. Velocipedes are also used in competitive sports such as road racing, mountain biking, and cyclocross.
